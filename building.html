Putting Together a PC

Putting together a PC is a fairly demanding proccess. Depending on the builder's ability and whether anythong goes wrong, it can take from 30 minutes to multiple hours. Below are essential videos that will go over in depth how to put the PC parts together, after you have bought them, and how to install Windows 10. Remember, after you build the PC, it won't do anything just yet. You need to plug it into an outlet, make sure the power supply is turned on, connect a screen or monitor and install an operating system, like Windows or Ubuntu. When connecting a monitor, note what kind of cable you are using, then find a matching port on your PC. It will either be on your GPU if you installed one, or on the motherboard if you don't have a GPU. Always plug the monitor into the GPU if you have one, or you won't be able to make use of it's power. Only after setting it up can you start using your new PC!

If you run into issues, or need to complete certain proccesses, YouTube is a great place to look, as are online search engines. A helpful tip for debugging issues is to type in an entry directly describing your problem, or copying and searching an error code you recieved.
